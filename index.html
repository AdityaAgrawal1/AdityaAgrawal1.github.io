Adobe SDE 1 Off-Campus Interview Experience 2022

Hello 👋 Everyone,

Hope you all are doing good. I am an Android developer with 1 year of experience. Recently I got the opportunity to get interviewed by Adobe for an SDE - 1 Android developer role for Acrobat team.

The interview was setup in 4 rounds.

  1. Technical Interview (Android)
  1. Technical Interview (Data Structures & Algorithms)
  1. Managerial round(Android + DSA + Behavioural)
  1. Managerial round(Android + DSA + Behavioral)

Round 1(Android)

Round 1 started with my introduction and later he asked me about one of my project. later he asked me question related to android.

  • MVVM vs MVP
  • Viewbinding vs Databinding? When to use what.
  • How Viewmodel survives configuration changes?
  • Write a api call for getting response from a backend server by following MVVM architecture.
  • About Coroutines
  • Livedata vs MutableLivedata? when to use what?
  • Activity Lifecycle? Lifecycle callbacks when app comes in splitscreen mode. How youtube app continues playback in splitscreen mode?
  • What is AsyncTask? How memory leak could be created using it? How do you resolve that?
  • What design patterns you have worked on? He asked me about singleton design pattern. Why to use singleton design pattern? Write a sample singleton class in Java. How would you resolve synchronization issue in singleton pattern? Later optimized the code using double checked locking.
  • What RxJava operator have you used? Difference between map and flatmap operator?

I answered all very well except I was not confident with Rxjava as I haven’t used that much.

Round 2(DSA)

This day I was very sick, Interviewer offered me to reschedule but I told her to continue my interview. It started with my Introduction. Later she asked a coding question related to

  • Linked list which was, to delete the last occurence of a number from the linked list [link]. I cleared some doubts first and later explained the approach and coded the solution.
  • Hashing Second question she asked was to find continous subarrays with sum zero [link]. I told the intituative approach and coded the solution. Later she asked to optimise the solution. So I was able to come closer to the solution, but could not reach the exact approach.

After 2 days, the recruiter informed me that I have been moved to the next round which would be managerial round.

Round 3(DSA + Android + Behavioural)

For this managerial round, I was expecting more questions of behavioural kind, but it was more of a technical interview. So It started with Interviewer introduction and later I introduced myself. He asked me about one of my projects. I explained him the project. The project was a lot of networking related and since the Interviewer was from some different backgroud , so a lot of things were new for him, but he was really impressed about my understanding of the project. Then he asked my thoughts on recursion vs iteration. I only answered this in terms of memory usage, but the interviewer also wanted to hear about the performance comparison between the two. Then he asked about coroutines and how it is different from Async task, I explained it well. Then he asked me questions about how will I make an Adaptive UI for mobile devices. Later he asked me question related to trees ( check whether the tree is symmetric or not), It was a simple question, so I explained the approach and later coded the solution. Then he asked a puzzle about time and distance, It was an easy one so I told the solution. Later he modified the puzzle two times and asked my thoughts so I explained the approach pretty well. He was satisfied with the approach. Later I asked some questions. While answering one of my question he gave me a hint that he is my Alumni. Later he asked how things are going in the department and some other college related talks happened.

Round 4(DSA + Android + Behavioural)

It started with her introduction and I introduced myself. She was very impressed by my introduction. She started asking questions about my personal project, How I am planning for the improvement of my certain feature in my project. After that she asked me the output of the program written in Java. I gave the solution and explained my thought process about my solution.

Then she asked me a coding question where I needed to find out the maximum no. of people in a party room given the arrival & departure time. I asked some clarification questions and started solving the question. But I messed up in this really simple question. I thought this question is similar to greedy algorithms (trains & platforms) question, so my mind started thinking in that way. But after trying 2 approaches I was not able to reach the solution. I wanted to try another approach but time was over. I felt really bad at that time. Later in the end I asked some questions. Also the interviewer identified me because I submitted improvement suggestion in the Adobe reader app. She appreciated that and ended the Interview saying that She really liked my side project Idea.

Same day I received the response, I was rejected. I tried contacting the manager for feedback, he said your last round was not upto the mark. I felt really sad because of just one simple question I was rejected. But it’s not the end, will try next time.

My Suggestions for someone appearing for Interview

  • Introduction definitely creates impact, so spend some time on it. Highlight your work, your qualities & cover what you do apart from coding.
  • Spend time on your personal growth. Learn something new every single day. Be passionate about learning(not necessarily coding, it could be anything).
  • Research about the company & its products and explore their products. Prepare what you like about it & what can you improve in that, this helps in answering behavioural questions.
  • Connect with people who joined recently to figure out what would be interview process like and also with people who are working for very long to find the working culture, this helps in projecting your growth in that company.
  • Ask questions after the interview to the interviewer, this shows you are interested and helps you in figuring out the working of the company and what you can expect in terms of your personal growth and learning.
